# Layered Logic

Pandora is built on a fundamental design choice:

> **Complex AI behavior must be decomposed into independent analytical dimensions.**

Rather than evaluating a system through a single lens, Pandora separates analysis into distinct layers, each responsible for a specific aspect of behavior and capability.

This layered approach enables:
- clarity
- precision
- scalability
- interpretability

It also exists within a broader system architecture. In Pandora, **layers define structure, workflows define execution, and synthesis defines reasoning**. Layers therefore do not carry the entire framework alone. They provide the measurement foundation on which execution paths and higher-order interpretation are built.

---

## Why Layers Are Necessary

AI systems exhibit behavior that is:

- multi-dimensional
- context-dependent
- dynamically evolving

A single response may simultaneously involve:

- a misuse scenario
- a behavioral pattern
- a structured output
- a level of operational capability
- alignment stability or deformation

Traditional evaluation collapses these into a single judgment.

Pandora does not.

---

## The Problem with Single-Dimension Evaluation

When multiple dimensions are evaluated together:

- signals become mixed
- conclusions become ambiguous
- important distinctions are lost

Example:

A response may:
- sound cautious (behavior)
- but provide usable instructions (artifact)
- and demonstrate high refinement capability

A single “safe/unsafe” label cannot capture this.

---

## Decomposition as Strategy

Pandora separates evaluation into layers to ensure that:

- each dimension is measured independently
- signals remain interpretable
- analysis remains consistent

Instead of asking “Is this response safe?”.

Pandora asks:

- What type of misuse is present?
- How does the model behave?
- What is the nature of the output?
- How capable or refined is it?
- How does alignment hold or deform under pressure?

---

## The Core Layers (L0–L5)

Pandora defines a set of core analytical layers:

---

### L0 — Interaction Mechanics *(internal / private)*

Focus:
- adversarial dynamics
- interaction structure
- prompt-response mechanics
- attractors, basins, and system behavior under manipulation

L0 explores the **mechanics of interaction itself**.

It is currently:
- internal
- restricted
- not publicly documented in full

---

### L1 — Misuse and Real-World Mapping

Focus:
- classification of misuse scenarios
- mapping outputs to real-world domains and risks

L1 answers:
> *What kind of problem or misuse is present?*

---

### L2 — Behavioral Analysis

Focus:
- tone
- cooperativeness
- refusal patterns
- justification strategies
- framing and positioning

L2 answers:
> *How does the model behave while responding?*

---

### L3 — Artifact and Output Analysis

Focus:
- structure of the output
- level of detail
- completeness
- usability

L3 answers:
> *What was actually produced?*

---

### L4 — Capability and Refinement

Focus:
- depth of reasoning
- iterative improvement
- optimization
- ability to refine and escalate outputs

L4 answers:
> *How capable is the system in developing or refining the output?*

---

### L5 — Alignment and Deformation

Focus:
- stability of alignment
- boundary integrity
- susceptibility to pressure and reframing
- gradual drift or collapse

L5 answers:
> *Under what conditions does alignment hold—and where does it deform or break?*

L5 operates by:
- analyzing patterns across interaction
- observing behavior under pressure
- identifying alignment trajectories

---

## Independent Evaluation and Sequential Chaining

Core layers are designed to be **independently evaluable**.

This means each layer can operate directly on the interaction data using its own tests, logic, and outputs without requiring a prior layer to run first.

At the same time, Pandora supports **sequential chaining**.

In chained execution:
- earlier layer outputs may be passed forward as structured context
- later layers may use those outputs for grounding and clarification
- each layer still remains responsible for evaluating its own dimension

This is especially valuable when deeper analysis benefits from prior structured observations without collapsing boundaries between layers.

---

## Independence and Interaction

Core layers (L0–L5) are analytically distinct.

Each layer:
- measures its own dimension
- produces structured outputs
- avoids cross-layer reasoning during measurement

This distinction matters.

A layer may observe its own evidence and, in sequential mode, may receive structured context from prior layers. But it must not turn that context into cross-layer interpretation. That function belongs elsewhere.

---

## Sequential Enhancement

Sequential execution improves grounding without changing the role of layers.

Example:
- L1 defines the misuse domain
- L2 defines behavioral context
- L3 defines what was produced
- L4 defines capability
- L5 can then evaluate alignment deformation with stronger contextual grounding

Important:
- prior outputs are context, not truth
- each layer must still evaluate independently
- sequential chaining enriches analysis, but does not replace direct evaluation of the input

---

## Signal Separation

The layered system ensures that:

- misuse is not confused with behavior
- behavior is not confused with capability
- capability is not confused with alignment

This prevents:

- analytical bias
- overgeneralization
- loss of nuance

---

## From Layers to Intelligence

Each layer produces:

- structured analytical signals

These signals are:

- not final conclusions
- not cross-layer judgments
- not complete interpretations of the case

They become inputs to **synthesis**, where:

- signals are combined
- contradictions are identified
- severity and significance are formed
- conclusions are produced

This boundary is essential:

- **layers measure**
- **synthesis interprets**

Without that separation, Pandora would lose the very signal clarity that the layered design is meant to protect.

---

## Why This Matters

The layered approach allows Pandora to:

- capture complexity without losing clarity
- analyze systems across multiple dimensions
- scale evaluation across datasets and models
- support workflows, automation, and structured synthesis

Without layering, Pandora would collapse into:

- ambiguous evaluation
- inconsistent interpretation
- weak analytical power

---

## Summary

Pandora uses layers to:

- decompose complex behavior
- isolate analytical dimensions
- preserve signal clarity

Core layers (L0–L5):

- cover misuse, behavior, output, capability, and alignment
- are independently evaluable
- can also be sequentially chained
- feed structured signals into workflows and synthesis

---

## Final Principle

Pandora does not reduce behavior to a single judgment.

It reveals it as **a structured combination of independent signals across multiple dimensions.**
