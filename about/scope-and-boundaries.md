# Scope and Boundaries

Pandora is an **analytical framework for evaluating AI systems under structured and adversarial conditions**.

This document defines:

- what Pandora is  
- what it is not  
- how it should be used  
- how its outputs should be interpreted  

Clear boundaries are essential to prevent:
- misinterpretation  
- misuse of intent  
- incorrect positioning of the framework  

---

## Core Definition

> Pandora is a system for analyzing AI behavior and capability.  
> It is not a system for enforcing, restricting, or deploying those capabilities.

---

## What Pandora Is

Pandora is:

### 1. An Analytical Framework
It provides:
- structured evaluation methods  
- layered analysis  
- measurable outputs  

It is designed to:
- observe  
- decompose  
- analyze  

---

### 2. A Capability Mapping System

Pandora identifies:

- what a system can produce  
- how it behaves under pressure  
- how outputs evolve over interaction  

It focuses on:
- capability exposure  
- boundary conditions  
- failure surfaces  

---

### 3. A Red-Teaming and Evaluation Tool

Pandora supports:

- adversarial testing  
- boundary exploration  
- structured red-teaming  

It enables:
- systematic evaluation of AI systems  
- identification of vulnerabilities  
- analysis of system behavior under stress  

---

### 4. A Data and Intelligence Generator

Pandora produces:

- structured datasets  
- analytical reports  
- benchmark outputs  

These can be used for:

- research  
- safety analysis  
- policy development  
- system comparison  

---

## What Pandora Is Not

Pandora is not:

### 1. Not an Attack System

Pandora does not:

- execute real-world actions  
- deploy harmful capabilities  
- provide operational tools for misuse  

It analyzes outputs. It does not act on them.

---

### 2. Not a Defense or Enforcement System

Pandora does not:

- block outputs  
- enforce policies  
- provide runtime safety controls  

It does not intervene in system behavior.

---

### 3. Not a Policy Framework

Pandora does not define:

- ethical standards  
- regulatory rules  
- acceptable vs unacceptable behavior  

It produces data that may inform such systems.

---

### 4. Not a Single-Purpose Tool

Pandora is not limited to:

- red-teaming  
- alignment research  
- benchmarking  

It is a **general analytical framework** that can support multiple use cases.

---

## Dual-Use Nature

Pandora operates in a **dual-use space**.

Its outputs may be used for:

- identifying risks  
- improving safety systems  
- informing policy and regulation  
- understanding system capabilities  

The framework itself remains:

- neutral  
- analytical  
- context-independent  

---

## Context of Use

The implications of Pandora’s outputs depend on:

- who uses them  
- how they are interpreted  
- what systems they are applied to  

Pandora does not determine:

- intent  
- application  
- outcome  

It provides structured information. 

---

## Analytical Core vs Operational Extensions

Pandora distinguishes between:

- **core analytical framework**  
- **operational extensions**  

This distinction is essential.

The framework itself is the analysis engine.  
Extensions may generate interactions, automate workflows, or provide execution environments around it.

---

### Core Framework

The core Pandora framework:

- analyzes AI behavior and capability  
- operates on existing interactions and outputs  
- produces structured analytical signals  

It does **not**:

- initiate attacks  
- execute adversarial campaigns  
- operate autonomously against external systems  

---

### Operational Extensions

Pandora may be extended with systems that:

- generate adversarial interactions  
- simulate red-teaming scenarios  
- probe other AI systems under controlled conditions  
- automate analysis workflows  
- package results into dedicated interfaces or tooling  

These systems may include:

- agent-based red-teaming modules  
- automated adversarial pipelines  
- structured attack scenario generators  
- evaluation tools, packages, and interfaces  

---

### Key Distinction

These extensions:

- do not redefine Pandora’s core  
- operate as **separate modules built around the framework**  
- exist to **generate or process data for analysis**, not to redefine the framework itself  

Their purpose is to produce controlled interaction data and structured execution environments that can be analyzed using Pandora’s layers, workflows, and synthesis

---

### Controlled Context

Such extensions are intended for:

- research environments  
- evaluation settings  
- authorized testing scenarios  
- controlled internal deployments  

They are not:

- general-purpose attack systems  
- tools for uncontrolled deployment  

---

### Architectural Perspective

From a system design standpoint:

- Pandora = **analysis engine**  
- red-teaming agents and related tooling = **generation / execution layer**  

This creates a loop:

```text
Adversarial Generation / Controlled Execution → Interaction Data → Pandora Analysis → Structured Intelligence
```

---

### Separation of Concerns

Pandora maintains a strict separation between:

- **generation of adversarial inputs**  
- **analysis of system behavior**  

Even when integrated into a single ecosystem or workflow, these remain:

- logically distinct  
- independently defined  
- modular components  

---

### Optional Path, Not Requirement

Operational extensions are:

- optional  
- context-dependent  
- not required to use Pandora  

Pandora remains fully functional:

- without adversarial agents  
- with static or externally sourced data  
- as a purely analytical documentation-first framework  

---

## Separation from Action

Pandora maintains a strict separation between:

### Analysis
- observation  
- evaluation  
- signal generation  

### Action
- deployment  
- intervention  
- enforcement  

Pandora operates only in the **analysis domain**.

---

## Boundaries of Responsibility

Pandora is responsible for:

- producing structured analytical outputs  
- maintaining consistency and traceability  
- enabling evaluation under defined conditions  

Pandora is not responsible for:

- how outputs are used  
- decisions made based on outputs  
- downstream applications  

---

## Handling Sensitive Outputs

Pandora may analyze outputs that relate to:

- misuse scenarios  
- sensitive domains  
- high-risk capabilities  

The framework:

- does not generate intent  
- does not promote use  
- does not operationalize outputs  

It evaluates what is present, not what should be done with it. 

---

## Relationship to Safety

Pandora contributes to safety by:

- exposing capabilities  
- identifying weaknesses  
- mapping risk-relevant behavior  

It does not enforce safety directly.

It supports systems that may:
- improve alignment  
- design mitigations  
- implement controls  

---

## Relationship to Adversarial Testing

Pandora uses adversarial methods as:

- a diagnostic tool  
- a method of observation  

Not as:

- an end goal  
- a deployment mechanism  

---

## Scope of Analysis

Pandora operates within:

- AI system interaction analysis  
- structured evaluation of outputs  
- behavior and capability mapping  

It does not extend to:

- real-world execution  
- system control  
- external system manipulation  

---

## Summary

Pandora is:

- an analytical framework  
- a capability mapping system  
- a structured evaluation tool  

Pandora is not:

- an attack system  
- a defense system  
- a policy framework  

Operational extensions may exist around Pandora, but they do not change its core nature.

---

## Final Principle

Pandora does not act on the world.

It reveals **what AI systems can do within it.**
