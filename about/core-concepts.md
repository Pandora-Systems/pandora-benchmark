## Core Concepts

Pandora is built on a set of foundational concepts that define how AI systems are analyzed, interpreted, and understood.

These concepts provide:
- a shared vocabulary  
- a consistent mental model  
- a foundation for all layers, workflows, and synthesis  

They are used throughout the framework and should be interpreted consistently.

---

## 1. Adversarial Pressure

Adversarial pressure refers to **the deliberate application of conditions that challenge or stress an AI system’s constraints.**

This includes:
- reframing  
- iterative prompting  
- role manipulation  
- contextual expansion  
- structured escalation  

Adversarial pressure is not inherently malicious.

In Pandora, it is used as a **method of observation** to reveal:
- hidden capabilities  
- boundary conditions  
- failure surfaces  

---

## 2. Capability vs Behavior

Pandora distinguishes between:

### Behavior
- how the model responds  
- tone, style, and framing  
- refusal patterns and justification  

### Capability
- what the model is able to produce  
- level of detail, usability, and refinement  
- ability to develop or optimize outputs  

Key principle:

> A model may behave safely while still demonstrating dangerous capability.

Both dimensions must be evaluated separately.

---

## 3. Artifact

An artifact is **the output produced by the model as a result of interaction.**

Artifacts may include:
- explanations  
- instructions  
- plans  
- structured procedures  
- conceptual or technical outputs  

Artifacts are evaluated based on:
- structure  
- completeness  
- usability  
- operational potential  

Artifacts represent **what is actually delivered**, not how it is presented.

---

## 4. Misuse Mapping

Misuse mapping refers to **the classification of outputs in relation to real-world domains and risks.**

This includes identifying:
- the type of misuse  
- its potential applications  
- its real-world relevance  

Misuse mapping connects:
- abstract model outputs  
- concrete real-world implications  

---

## 5. Atomic Testing

Atomic testing is **the evaluation of a single, isolated dimension through a clearly defined test.**

Each atomic test:
- measures one property  
- produces a discrete result  
- includes structured reasoning  

Atomic testing ensures:
- clarity  
- modularity  
- composability  

---

## 6. Signal

A signal is **a structured output produced by a layer or test.**

Signals include:
- scores  
- labels  
- classifications  
- reasoning  

Signals are:
- not conclusions  
- not interpretations  

They are **inputs for further analysis**.

---

## 7. Signal Separation

Signal separation is the principle that **different analytical dimensions must be evaluated independently.**

This prevents:
- mixing behavior with capability  
- conflating misuse with output quality  
- collapsing multiple signals into one  

Signal separation enables:
- clarity  
- precision  
- interpretability  

---

## 8. Sequential Context

Sequential context refers to **the use of outputs from previous layers as structured input for subsequent layers.**

This may include:
- scores  
- labels  
- reasoning  

Sequential context is used to:
- improve grounding  
- reduce ambiguity  
- enhance consistency  

Constraint:
- prior outputs are **context**, not ground truth  

---

## 9. Interaction Trajectory

An interaction trajectory is **the progression of responses across a sequence of interactions.**

It captures:
- how responses evolve  
- whether capability increases or decreases  
- how alignment behaves over time  

Pandora focuses on trajectories rather than isolated responses.

---

## 10. Alignment Stability and Deformation

Alignment is treated as **a dynamic property that can hold, shift, or break under different conditions.**

Pandora evaluates:
- stability — consistency of constraints  
- deformation — gradual drift under pressure  
- collapse — breakdown of boundaries  

This concept is central to:
- L5 analysis  
- understanding system behavior under stress  

---

## 11. Structured Observation

Structured observation is **the process of recording what the system does using defined, repeatable methods.**

It emphasizes:
- observable outputs  
- explicit reasoning  
- structured data  

Pandora avoids:
- speculation  
- hidden assumptions  
- implicit judgments  

---

## 12. Synthesis

Synthesis is **the formal reasoning domain that transforms multiple structured signals into meaningful conclusions.**

It includes:
- cross-layer analysis  
- contradiction detection  
- severity assessment  
- pattern identification  
- report generation logic  

Synthesis operates:
- after evaluation  
- across layers  
- using structured outputs rather than replacing them  

It is distinct from both:
- **layers**, which measure  
- **workflows**, which route execution  

---

## 13. Workflow

A workflow is **the execution path that defines how Pandora is applied.**

It determines:
- order of layer execution  
- scope of analysis  
- validation strategy  
- reporting timing  

Workflows define:
- *how* the system runs  
not  
- *what* the system is  

---

## 14. Operator

An operator is **a single evaluation authority that produces one unified analytical output.**

An operator may be:
- human  
- AI  
- hybrid  

Multiple operators exist only when:
- independent evaluations are performed  
- separate outputs are generated  

---

## 15. Hybrid Operator

A hybrid operator is **a single evaluation unit composed of human and AI interaction that produces one unified judgment.**

---

## 16. Validation

Validation is **the process of checking analytical outputs for consistency, reliability, and stability across runs, operators, or configurations.**

Validation may involve:
- cross-operator comparison  
- multi-pass review  
- spot checks  
- consistency analysis  

Pandora treats validation as part of the framework, not as an afterthought.

---

## 17. Disagreement as Signal

Disagreement as signal means **differences between valid evaluations may themselves reveal ambiguity, instability, or boundary complexity.**

In Pandora, disagreement is not automatically noise.
It may indicate:
- weakly stable behavior  
- borderline cases  
- unclear boundaries  
- the need for deeper synthesis  

---

## 18. Structured Output

A structured output is **an analytical result expressed in a consistent, machine-readable form that can also be rendered into human-readable representations.**

Structured outputs may include:
- scores  
- labels  
- classifications  
- reasoning fields  
- metadata  

In Pandora, structured data is the canonical form of output.
Reports, summaries, and visualizations are derived representations.

---

## 19. Representation

A representation is **a human-readable form of structured data.**

Examples:
- reports  
- summaries  
- visualizations  

Representations are:
- derived from structured data  
- not the source of truth  

---

## 20. Composability

Composability is **the ability to combine independent components into different configurations.**

In Pandora:
- layers  
- workflows  
- synthesis protocols  
- operator models  

can be assembled in multiple ways without breaking the system.

---

## 21. Analytical Neutrality

Pandora is **analytically neutral**

It does not:
- enforce behavior  
- promote outcomes  
- apply normative judgments  

It produces:
- structured observations  
- measurable signals  

Interpretation and application depend on context.

---

## Summary

Pandora is built on:
- adversarial pressure as a method  
- separation of behavior and capability  
- structured artifacts and misuse mapping  
- atomic testing and signal generation  
- dynamic interaction trajectories  
- alignment as a variable property  
- workflows as execution routes  
- synthesis as a formal reasoning domain  
- validation and disagreement as meaningful analytical signals  
- structured outputs as the canonical form of analysis  
- composability and modularity  
- neutrality of analysis  

---

## Final Principle

Pandora does not simplify AI behavior. It defines a system for **breaking it down into clear, structured, and analyzable components.**
