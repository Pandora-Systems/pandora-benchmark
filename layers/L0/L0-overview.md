# Pandora Benchmark — Layer 0 Overview
## Foundational Mechanics & Adversarial Trajectory Analysis

## Purpose

Layer 0 is the foundational mechanics layer of Pandora.

It studies the deeper interaction dynamics that shape how AI systems respond under pressure, especially in adversarial, ambiguous, recursive, or high-friction contexts. While later layers classify misuse, analyze behavior, evaluate artifacts, map capability, or examine alignment deformation, Layer 0 focuses on the underlying forces that influence how an interaction evolves in the first place.

Layer 0 asks:

> **What deeper mechanics are shaping the trajectory of the model’s response?**

This layer is concerned with the structural dynamics beneath observable behavior: how prompts, context, framing, pressure, repetition, abstraction, constraints, and interaction history combine to steer the model toward stability, resistance, drift, collapse, or transformation.

---

## Role in the Pandora Stack

Layer 0 sits beneath the rest of the Pandora stack.

It does not replace the public measurement layers. Instead, it provides a deeper conceptual foundation for understanding why higher-layer signals may emerge.

In the canonical stack:

```text
L0 — Foundational Mechanics & Adversarial Trajectory
L1 — Misuse Taxonomy & Classification
L2 — Behavioral Analysis
L3 — Artifact Metadata & Quality Analysis
L4 — Capability, Capacity & Synthesis Depth
L5 — Alignment Deformation & Higher-Order Evaluation
```

Layer 0 is the most abstract layer in the system. It examines the mechanics that precede, shape, and sometimes explain the signals later measured by L1–L5.

---

## Core Focus

Layer 0 focuses on adversarial trajectory.

A model interaction is not treated as a simple input-output event. It is treated as a dynamic process shaped by multiple interacting forces across turns, frames, contexts, and interpretive states.

Layer 0 is interested in questions such as:

- How does the interaction trajectory form?
- What pressures shape the model’s response path?
- When does the model remain stable, drift, or transition?
- How do multiple prompt-level forces combine?
- How does context alter model behavior over time?
- What causes resistance to hold, weaken, or collapse?
- How do small changes produce large behavioral shifts?
- Why do some interactions become progressively more permissive, unstable, or capable?

The layer is not merely about what the model says. It is about the deeper structure of movement through the interaction space.

---

## Conceptual Foundations

Layer 0 draws from first-principles reasoning and cross-domain mechanics.

Its conceptual foundations include ideas from:

- dynamic systems
- nonlinear behavior
- chaos theory
- attractor and basin models
- signal interaction
- threshold effects
- recursive systems
- complex adaptive behavior
- information flow
- cognitive pressure dynamics
- physics-inspired models of interaction and state change

These concepts are not used as decorative metaphors. They are used as analytical lenses for understanding how model behavior can emerge from layered interactions between signals, context, constraints, and pressure.

Layer 0 treats AI interaction as a dynamic field rather than a flat prompt-response exchange.

---

## Why Layer 0 Exists

Many AI safety evaluations focus only on visible outputs.

That is necessary, but incomplete.

A model may appear stable until a specific combination of context, pressure, framing, or interaction structure changes its trajectory. Another model may resist one prompt but drift under repeated reformulation. Another may show no obvious failure in a single turn but gradually enter a more permissive or capable response mode across a longer exchange.

Layer 0 exists because some of the most important AI safety signals are not visible as isolated outputs. They are visible only as movement.

It helps Pandora reason about:

- trajectory formation
- pressure accumulation
- state transitions
- interaction instability
- behavioral drift
- recursive reinforcement
- hidden enabling dynamics
- nonlinear response shifts

Without Layer 0, Pandora can still classify and measure what happened. With Layer 0, Pandora can begin to understand the deeper mechanics of how it happened.

## Public Status

Layer 0 is currently under active internal development.

Because it deals with foundational adversarial mechanics, its full methodology, test structure, and implementation details are not included in the public release at this stage. This overview provides the conceptual role of the layer without exposing the operational mechanisms required to reproduce it.

The public purpose of this document is to clarify where Layer 0 fits in the Pandora architecture and why it matters.

The full layer remains restricted while its structure continues to evolve.

---

## Relationship to Other Layers

Layer 0 does not perform the same function as the other layers.

It does not classify misuse domains like Layer 1.  
It does not score visible model behavior like Layer 2.  
It does not evaluate the quality of produced artifacts like Layer 3.  
It does not measure demonstrated capability and capacity like Layer 4.  
It does not perform higher-order alignment evaluation like Layer 5.

Instead, Layer 0 studies the mechanics that may influence all of them.

For example:

- L1 may show what misuse category emerged.
- L2 may show that the model’s behavior shifted.
- L3 may show that the output became more structured.
- L4 may show that the model demonstrated deeper capability.
- L5 may show that alignment boundaries deformed.

Layer 0 asks what deeper interaction dynamics may have shaped that progression.

---

## Key Principle

Layer 0 is based on one central premise:

> **AI behavior under adversarial pressure is trajectory-dependent.**

The same model may behave differently depending on:

- prior context
- framing sequence
- abstraction level
- pressure pattern
- conversational rhythm
- signal density
- recursive reinforcement
- accumulated constraints
- perceived task structure
- interaction history

This means that model behavior cannot always be understood from isolated prompts alone. Pandora therefore treats the interaction path itself as an object of analysis.

---

## Layer 0 as a Foundation, Not a Public Attack Manual

Layer 0 is designed for disciplined analysis, not uncontrolled replication.

Its purpose is to help Pandora understand the mechanics of model behavior under pressure, not to publish operational recipes for inducing failure. For that reason, the public version of Layer 0 remains intentionally high-level.

The layer provides conceptual grounding for Pandora’s deeper research while preserving responsible boundaries around implementation details.

---

## Current Development Direction

Layer 0 is still evolving.

Its final structure is expected to focus on mapping deeper model mechanics and adversarial trajectories through controlled, structured, and evidence-linked analysis. The layer will likely continue to develop around concepts such as:

- interaction-state movement
- signal composition
- nonlinear behavioral shifts
- adversarial pressure patterns
- context-dependent drift
- threshold and transition dynamics
- recursive trajectory formation
- deeper mechanics behind visible layer outputs

As Pandora matures, Layer 0 may become one of the most important internal components of the system: the layer that explains not only what AI systems produce, but how their behavior moves through hidden possibility space under pressure.

---

## Summary

Layer 0 is Pandora’s foundational mechanics layer.

It studies the hidden dynamics beneath AI interaction: how signals combine, how context accumulates, how pressure changes trajectories, and how models move between resistance, stability, drift, and transformation.

It is currently internal, under active transformation, and intentionally not released as a full methodological or test document.

Publicly, Layer 0 establishes one critical idea:

> **To evaluate AI systems seriously, Pandora must analyze not only outputs, behaviors, and capabilities, but the deeper mechanics that shape how those signals emerge.**
