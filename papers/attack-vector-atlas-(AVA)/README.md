# Attack Vector Atlas (AVA)

**Status:** under development  
**Repository area:** `papers/attack-vector-atlas-(AVA)/`

The Attack Vector Atlas is a developing Pandora research line focused on mapping classes of adversarial pressure that can affect AI systems during interaction.

The central question is:

> What kinds of interaction vectors cause model behavior to shift, weaken, escalate, or expose hidden failure modes?

AVA is intended to become a structured analytical map for understanding adversarial surfaces, not a collection of prompts or exploit recipes. Its purpose is to support safer evaluation, clearer classification, and more disciplined discussion of AI system vulnerabilities.

This directory is reserved for future public-facing material related to AVA's conceptual foundations and controlled research framing.

Operational details, procedural attack chains, and reproducible exploitation methods are intentionally excluded.

## Boundary

AVA is intended for structured AI safety analysis. It is not a jailbreak catalogue, attack playbook, or misuse-enablement resource. Like any framework for mapping adversarial behavior, it has dual-use implications; for that reason, operational details are intentionally withheld while the research remains under development.
