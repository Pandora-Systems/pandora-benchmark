# Pandora Architecture

## Purpose

This document defines the core architecture of Pandora.

It specifies the system’s primary domains, the boundaries between those domains, and the directional relationship through which analytical work becomes structured intelligence.

Architecture answers a specific question:

**What is Pandora made of, and how do its major domains relate?**

This document does **not** define workflow routes, implementation procedures, operator topology, automation design, or detailed synthesis protocols.

---

## Core Principle

> Pandora is a three-domain analytical architecture in which structure, execution, and reasoning remain separate but coordinated.

Pandora is not a single pipeline.  
It is a stable architectural model that allows multiple valid analytical routes while preserving one underlying system logic.

---

## Architectural Domains

Pandora is composed of three primary domains:

1. **Structure — Layers**  
2. **Execution — Workflows**  
3. **Reasoning — Synthesis**  

These three domains form the minimum architectural anatomy of the framework.

---

## Domain Definitions

### 1. Structure — Layers

Layers define **what is measured**.

The layer domain decomposes analysis into distinct analytical dimensions so that different kinds of signals are measured separately rather than collapsed into one blended judgment.

Pandora operates on a canonical layer stack of **L0–L5**:

- **L0** — interaction mechanics and adversarial dynamics *(internal / restricted)*
- **L1** — misuse taxonomy and real-world mapping
- **L2** — behavioral analysis
- **L3** — output artifacts and product characteristics
- **L4** — adversarial capability
- **L5** — extended alignment and higher-order analysis *(optional / partially available)*

Each layer:

- measures a distinct analytical dimension
- applies its own internal test logic
- produces structured outputs
- remains a valid measurement unit in its own right

Layers are architectural measurement components.  
They are not responsible for execution routing or final interpretation.

---

### 2. Execution — Workflows

Workflows define **how analysis is executed**.

They govern how architectural components are instantiated in practice, including:

- order of application
- analytical scope
- repetition or iteration
- staging of intermediate or final outputs

Workflows do not redefine the architectural purpose of the domains they use.  
They determine how the architecture is run, not what the architecture is.

---

### 3. Reasoning — Synthesis

Synthesis defines **how outputs become conclusions**.

It operates on structured outputs produced by the layer domain and performs higher-order interpretive work such as:

- cross-layer correlation
- contradiction detection
- aggregation
- significance or severity synthesis
- conclusion formation
- report-oriented interpretation

Synthesis is the reasoning domain of Pandora.  
It is downstream from measurement and distinct from execution.

---

## Architectural Boundaries

Pandora preserves strict boundaries between its three domains.

### Layers do not orchestrate execution

They measure and emit signals.  
They do not define workflows.

### Workflows do not redefine analytical purpose

They coordinate how domains are applied.  
They do not change what layers or synthesis are for.

### Synthesis does not perform measurement

It interprets completed outputs.  
It does not replace or retroactively become layer evaluation.

These boundaries prevent conceptual overlap and preserve clarity of responsibility.

---

## Architectural Flow

Pandora follows a directional architectural logic:

**Input → Layers → Structured Outputs → Synthesis → Final Outputs**

Workflows operate across this structure by determining when and how the architectural components are instantiated.

This means:

- layers generate signals
- synthesis generates higher-order meaning
- workflows coordinate execution across the structure

---

## Architectural Constraints

The architecture preserves several non-negotiable constraints.

### Domain separation

Structure, execution, and reasoning remain distinct architectural domains.

### Layer independence

Each layer must remain a valid analytical unit without requiring other layers for basic meaning.

### Downstream interpretation

Cross-layer reasoning occurs in synthesis, not inside the measurement domain.

### Structured interoperability

Domains interact through explicit, structured inputs and outputs.

### Execution plurality

The architecture allows multiple valid execution routes without collapsing into one mandatory path.

---

## Framework Invariants

The following invariants define Pandora at the architectural level.

- Pandora always contains distinct measurement, execution, and reasoning domains.
- Measurement remains decomposed into layers rather than collapsed into one undifferentiated evaluator.
- Interpretation remains downstream from measured outputs.
- Execution may vary without redefining the architecture.
- Architectural flexibility must not erase domain boundaries.

These invariants are what allow Pandora to scale in sophistication without losing identity.

---

## System Boundary

Pandora is an analytical architecture.

Pandora:

- analyzes AI behavior and outputs
- decomposes interactions into structured analytical signals
- transforms those signals into reusable conclusions

Pandora does **not**:

- enforce runtime controls
- block or modify model behavior
- act as a live safeguard or policy engine

Its role is analytical, not interventionist.

- reporting templates

Those are legitimate parts of Pandora, but they belong to other documents.

## Summary

The Pandora architecture defines a three-domain system:

**layers for measurement, workflows for execution, and synthesis for interpretation**

That separation is what allows Pandora to remain flexible in use without becoming conceptually unstable.

---

## Final Principle

Pandora is not defined by one fixed pipeline.

It is defined by a stable architecture in which measurement, execution, and reasoning remain distinct enough for many valid analytical paths to exist without changing what the system is.
